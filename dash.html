What is Interim?

Interim is a weeklong, off-campus learning experience for all four years of high school. Students select from a range of experiences, guided by the HKIS Vision statement and structured around four attributes: Engagement, Challenge, relationships and Skills. Interim forms an integral part of the high school curriculum and participation is a graduation requirement for all high school students.

Purpose and Goals

Interim is a carefully constructed program that presents students with challenges and opportunities for personal growth. The Interim experience is designed to help students:

  • Build relationships and interactions with other students and faculty members.
  • Cultivate a spirit of community.
  • Expand exposure to diverse cultures and traditions.
  • Work as a member of a team.
  • Develop communication and leadership skills, self-reliance and compassion.
  • Venture out of their physical, emotional and intellectual comfort zones.
  • Discover new talents and interests.
  • Gain greater self-knowledge and a richer understanding of the world around them.
  • Grow in responsibility and independence.

The experience of Interim compels students to draw upon the values they have developed at home and school as they encounter new situations and novel challenges. Students emerge from the program with a more profound sense of self, an enriched appreciation of human diversity, and deeper insight into the faith, traditions and cultural values that inspire compassion and humility. Ultimately, participation in the program grants students the very real satisfaction that they have taken responsibility for their own learning.

Communication Policy

We encourage students to be immersed in their Interim experience, so we recommend that Interim sponsors collect student cell phones during the day’s activities. We appreciate your support with this, and ask that you do not contact your child directly while they are engaged in activities.

Parent contact for emergency communications during Interim week should be through Ms Lauren Fine, Associate Principal for Student Life. Ms. Fine will be in regular contact with teachers providers throughout the week of Interim and she will update the Interim websites with information as necessary.

If there are any important parent communications for the student, please contact Ms. Fine and she will relay the information through the trip sponsor. Similarly, if there are any important communications from the child, they will be instructed to relay the information through the trip sponsor who will be in contact with Ms. Fine.

Pre-Interim

Positive interaction among students, faculty, guides and local people is a hallmark of all Interim courses. It is unacceptable for students to inconvenience others, or jeopardize the safety of the group. Students must demonstrate to their Interim sponsors that they are well suited, adequately prepared, and will be supportive of the group during the duration of Interim week. If, during pre-Interim meetings and training sessions, students have not demonstrated adequate preparation and responsible behavior, they may be reassigned to a different Interim option. In such cases, additional expenses may be incurred by parents.

Pre-Interim meetings:

Students are required to attend all scheduled pre-Interim meetings and any mandatory training sessions. Failure to attend will jeopardize a student's participation in the desired course and they may be reassigned to another Interim at parent expense.

During Interim week, students are expected to adhere to all high school rules as stated in the Student Handbook. ACTIVITIES, EQUIPMENT AND PROCEDURES WITHIN EACH COURSE ARE CATEGORIZED INTO ONE OF FOUR RISK LEVELS:

Level 1: Low risk activities in which:
  • Students have received teacher approval to operate the equipment or proceed with the activity, and
  • Students will not be hindered or impeded while using the equipment or engaging in the activity.
Level 2: Medium risk activities in which:
  • There is a degree of risk of injury to a student using this equipment or engaging in this activity to the extent that all the risk cannot be eliminated;
  • Students require a safe working space to be able to operate the equipment or engage in the activity; and
  • Personal protective equipment may be required to further reduce the risk of injury.
Level 3: High risk activities in which:
  • There is a high risk of injury to the student or other persons if the equipment or procedures described in the activity are not employed in the prescribed manner;
  • The teacher should maintain close supervision of all aspects of this activity; and
  • There is a need for the teacher and/or student to operate in a defined area.
Level 4: Very high risk activities in which:
  • There is a high risk of injury to the student or other persons if the equipment or procedures described in the activity are not employed in the prescribed manner;
  • An injury sustained during this activity could result in a permanent disability or death;
  • A high level of supervision by the teacher is required (a maximum of one student at any one time working with teacher guidance may be appropriate); and
  • The activity is undertaken in a defined, restricted area.

SAFETY INFORMATION

The nature and purpose of our Interim courses may expose students to a degree of risk not typical of regular on-campus classes or activities. While courses involving physical activity and exploration are not risk-free, physical safety is of paramount importance as courses are planned and take place. All trips are rated on an Activity Risk Level to help students and parents choose appropriate trips.

The following guidelines are in place for Interim trips with certain activities:

  • Certain adventure trips might require a diagnostic hike, test for cycling competency or fitness level.
  • Interim courses that include water activities (SCUBA diving, kayaking, snorkeling, surfing, sailing etc), students must:
    • pass a minimum 25-meter swimming test
    • spend appropriate ‘in-pool’ training time prior to departure
  • Attendance at training sessions may be mandatory - please check individual trip information pages for more information.

Interim teachers carry a first aid kit that contains typical items that might be needed. For more serious medical issues, emergency evacuation is available.

Some Interim courses may have additional medical requirements. Itineraries that require rigorous physical exercise or special skills may also require mandatory pre-trip training or assessment.

As Interim sponsors need to be aware of contact information, any medical issues, physical conditions or allergies, parents are required to keep all family details and medical information updated on Powerschool (after logging in, look on the top left side). Completion of an additional medical form with any current and temporary information is required just prior to Interim week.
